# 1. FINDING TRIAL CHAMBERS

## GENERATION PATTERNS

Trial Chambers are massive underground structures that generate in the **Deep Dark** biome, typically below Y=0. These aren't random pockets - the ancients built these facilities with purpose.

### Location Markers

- **Surface Indicators:** Look for large circular openings in swamp or jungle biomes
- **Cave Connections:** Trial Chambers often connect to large cave systems
- **Depth Range:** Most chambers generate between Y=-20 and Y=-40
- **Size Scale:** We're talking cathedral-sized underground arenas

### Discovery Protocol

1. **Set your LLM Task Planner** to "locate_trial_chamber"
2. **Equip:** Standard exploration gear (see Section 7)
3. **Survey Method:** Spiral descent from Y=0, listening for trial spawner acoustics
4. **Mapping:** Steve Agents should chart chamber layouts for future reference

```
CREW TALK: THE FIRST FIND

Jim: "Forty levels down and I'm seein' something through this mossy cobble. Big room, lots of redstone."

Sarah: "That's it. Trial Chamber. Your pick's about to hit trial spawner territory. Get your sword ready."

Jim: "Thought we were mining?"

Sarah: "We are, Jim. We're mining OPPORTUNITY."
```

# 3. TRIAL SPAWNERS: HOW THEY WORK

## MECHANISM OVERVIEW

Trial Spawners aren't your typical dungeon spawners. These are sophisticated combat testing devices that adapt to crew strength.

### Core Mechanics

- **Player Detection:** Activates when crew enters proximity range (16 blocks)
- **Occupancy Sensing:** Scales spawn count based on nearby crew members
- **Difficulty Adjustment:** More crew = harder waves = better rewards
- **Cooldown Period:** Inactive between trials (recovery time)

### Difficulty Tiers

```
CREW TALK: SPAWNER LOGIC

Mike (Steve Agent): "Task Planner indicates this spawner will spawn 12 hostiles for our crew size."

Sarah: "Perfect. That means we hit the sweet spot. Any fewer and we're wasting time. Any more and Jim's gonna need more armor."

Jim: "I heard that."

Sarah: "Jim, your armor efficiency is 67%. That's 'acceptable', not 'good'. Stay behind Mike."
```

### Spawn Patterns

1. **Initial Wave:** Scouts and light combatants
2. **Escalation:** Heavier mobs, mixed types
3. **Boss Wave:** Rare champions or Breeze encounters
4. **Completion:** Spawner enters cooldown, rewards available

# 4. THE BREEZE: NEW THREAT PROFILE

## MOB INTELLIGENCE REPORT

The Breeze is a unique hostile mob exclusive to Trial Chambers. It's not just another monster - it's a tactical puzzle.

### Breeze Characteristics

| Attribute | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Health** | 30 HP (15 hearts) | Moderate durability |
| **Attack** | 7-9 damage | Significant per hit |
| **Speed** | High | Evasive movement |
| **Attack Type** | Wind burst projectiles | Ranged knockback |
| **Behavior** | Tactical positioning | Uses terrain |

### Combat Tactics

**Movement Pattern:**
- Breeze constantly repositions after attacks
- Uses walls and corners for cover
- Can leap over obstacles to escape

**Attack Profile:**
- **Wind Burst:** Ranged projectile with knockback
- **Burst Spread:** Can hit multiple targets
- **Cooldown:** 3-4 seconds between attacks

**Recommended Countermeasures:**
1. **Shield Blocks:** Standard blocking reduces wind burst damage by 50%
2. **Corner Tactics:** Force Breeze into confined spaces
3. **Ranged Priority:** Bows/crossbows before they close distance
4. **Mobility:** Don't get cornered yourself

```
CREW TALK: BREEZE ENCOUNTER

Jim: "Okay, target locked. Breeze incoming."

Sarah: "Mike, flank right. Jim, hold position with shield. I've got high ground."

Mike: "Breeze is evasive. Standard targeting algorithms ineffective."

Sarah: "Override. Manual tracking. Predict jump patterns. Jim, when it vaults, THAT'S your shot window."

Jim: "Copy. Wind burst incoming... BLOCKED. Counterfire..."

Sarah: "Clean hit. That's how we do it, MineWright."
```

### Breeze Drops

- **Wind Charges:** Can be used for wind burst attacks
- **Breeze Rods:** Crafting material for unique weapons
- **Experience:** Significantly higher than standard mobs

# 6. KEY & VAULT SYSTEM

## OMINOUS TRIAL PROTOCOL

Standard trials provide standard rewards. Ominous trials provide MINIMUM wage for the danger you face. Here's how to unlock the good stuff.

### Trial Keys

**Acquisition Methods:**
- **Ominous Trial Spawners:** Defeat an ominous variant for guaranteed key
- **Breeze Kills:** 25% drop chance per Breeze defeated
- **Chamber Chests:** Rare spawn in hidden side rooms
- **Bartering:** Some trial chambers have villager trading posts

**Key Types:**
- **Standard Trial Key:** Opens basic vaults
- **Ominous Trial Key:** Opens ominous vaults (better loot)
- **Master Key:** (Rumored) Opens all vaults in a chamber

### Vault Mechanics

**Access Protocol:**
1. **Locate Vault:** Usually at chamber end or hidden areas
2. **Insert Key:** Consumed on use
3. **Loot Generation:** Instant reward distribution
4. **Vault Reset:** Some vaults regenerate after cooldown

**Vault Identification:**
- **Standard Vault:** Copper/iron trim, basic lock
- **Ominous Vault:** Redstone trim, ominous particle effects
- **Reward Vault:** Gold trim, post-trial placement

# 8. TEAM TRIAL RUNS: MULTI-AGENT COORDINATION

## CREW OPERATIONS

Trial Chambers scale with crew size. One Steve Agent can handle entry-level trials. For serious operations, you need coordination.

### Role Assignments

#### Tank Role
**Primary:** Aggro management, shield blocking
**Secondary:** Spawn point control
**Equipment:** Highest armor, shield, knockback resistance
**AI Behavior:** Defensive positioning, threat prioritization

#### Damage Role
**Primary:** Ranged attack, Breeze targeting
**Secondary:** Loot collection between waves
**Equipment:** Crossbow, Power bow, high damage output
**AI Behavior:** Target priority, mobile positioning

#### Support Role
**Primary:** Potion distribution, emergency healing
**Secondary:** Chest management, resource organization
**Equipment:** Healing supplies, empty inventory space
**AI Behavior:** Crew monitoring, responsive healing

### Communication Protocol

**Pre-Trial:**
1. **Confirm Roles:** Each Agent knows their assignment
2. **Mark Rally Point:** Designated meeting location
3. **Set Emergency Beacon:** Safe zone for retreat

**During Trial:**
1. **Status Updates:** Health, ammo, enemy count
2. **Target Calling:** Priority targets announced
3. **Position Reports:** Real-time location sharing

**Post-Trial:**
1. **Loot Distribution:** Fair allocation based on role
2. **Damage Assessment:** Equipment durability check
3. **Next Trial Decision:** Continue or retreat?

```
CREW TALK: ROLE COORDINATION

Sarah: "Alright team, Trial Chamber layout confirmed. We've got three waves, Breeze at end."

Mike: "I'll take tank. My armor durability is at 89%."

Sarah: "Good. Jim, you're on damage. That Breeze rod we found last run? Put it on your crossbow."

Jim: "Already mounted. Wind burst capability confirmed."

Sarah: "I'll handle loot and potions. Everyone set your rally point at the corridor entrance. If things go south, we regroup there."

Mike: "Trial Spawner activating. Wave 1 incoming."

Sarah: "MineWright, we work. Let's earn that vault key."
```

### Spatial Tactics

**Formation:**
- **Triangle Formation:** Tank at point, damage and support flanking
- **Chokepoint Usage:** Funnel enemies into kill zones
- **High Ground:** Whenever possible (ranged advantage)

**Breeze-Specific Tactics:**
- **Flank Attack:** Two damage Agents from different angles
- **Corridor Trap:** Lure Breeze into confined space
- **Shield Wall:** Tank blocks wind bursts, damage returns fire

# 9. RESET MECHANICS & FARMING POTENTIAL

## SUSTAINABLE OPERATIONS

Can you farm Trial Chambers? Sort of. Here's what you need to know about long-term operations.

### Spawner Cooldowns

**Trial Spawners:**
- **Base Cooldown:** 30 minutes between trials
- **Occupancy Reset:** Moving away and back resets detection
- **Difficulty Reset:** Returns to base difficulty after cooldown

**Respawn Mechanics:**
- **Chamber Mobs:** Do NOT respawn naturally
- **Spawner Drops:** Repeated trials, repeated rewards
- **Vault Access:** One-time per key, but keys are renewable

### Farming Strategies

#### Single-Chamber Farming
1. **Clear All Hostiles:** Complete initial trial
2. **Loot Collection:** Gather everything, clear inventory
3. **Wait For Cooldown:** 30 minutes or explore nearby areas
4. **Return:** Spawner resets, ready for another run

**Pros:**
- Controlled environment
- Predictable encounters
- Safe retreat distance

**Cons:**
- Limited loot per hour
- Time inefficient
- Boredom factor

#### Multi-Chamber Routes
1. **Map Network:** Connect multiple Trial Chambers
2. **Sequential Runs:** Complete each chamber in rotation
3. **Continuous Loot:** No waiting for single spawner cooldown

**Pros:**
- Maximum loot per hour
- Variety in encounters
- Efficient use of time

**Cons:**
- Dangerous travel between chambers
- Complex navigation
- Higher resource cost

### Loot Sustainability

**Renewable Resources:**
- Wind Charges (via Breeze kills)
- Experience (always renewable)
- Iron/Gold (from mob drops)

**Limited Resources:**
- Trial Keys (must earn new ones)
- Ominous Keys (Breeze drops only)
- Unique Enchanted Books (finite per chamber)

# 10. SAFETY PROTOCOLS

## EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

Trial Chambers are dangerous. Even with perfect preparation, things go wrong. Here's how we handle emergencies.

### Crew Down Protocol

**Agent Casualty:**
1. **Immediate Withdrawal:** Remaining crew retreat to rally point
2. **Inventory Secure:** Dead Agent's drops are collected if safe
3. **Regroup:** Assess remaining combat capability
4. **Decision:** Continue with reduced crew OR full retreat

**Total Wipe:**
1. **Accept Loss:** Gear is gone. Don't make it worse.
2. **Mark Location:** Coordinates for recovery run
3. **Return Prepared:** Better gear, backup Agents
4. **Learn:** Analyze what went wrong

### Environmental Hazards

**Deep Dark Darkness:**
- **Light Level:** Keep torches placed
- **Sculk Sensors:** Avoid unnecessary vibrations
- **Warden Risk:** Don't trigger sensors near chambers

**Trial Spawner Traps:**
- **Surprise Spawns:** Always expect backup
- **Environmental Damage:** Some spawners trigger lava/arrow traps
- **Mob Combos:** Multiple types can spawn simultaneously

### Extraction Protocol

**Emergency Exit:**
1. **Designated Escape Route:** Identified before trial start
2. **Cobblestone Walls:** Block enemy pursuit if necessary
3. **Rendezvous Point:** Above-ground rally location
4. **Return to Base:** Don't push your luck

```
CREW TALK: EXTRACTION

Mike: "Agent Jim is down. Inventory dropped at coordinates: [-312, -42, 894]"

Sarah: "Cause of death?"

Mike: "Breeze wind burst knockback into lava. Failed shield block."

Mike: "Affirmative. Hostiles cleared in sector 4. Path is open."
```
